Lasagna

Lasagna is a classic Italian dish renowned for its layers of wide, flat pasta, rich sauces, and savory fillings. Each layer typically alternates between pasta sheets, a hearty meat or vegetable ragu, creamy béchamel or ricotta cheese mixture, and a generous sprinkling of grated cheese, usually mozzarella and Parmesan. The dish is assembled in a baking dish and cooked until the top is golden and bubbly, allowing the flavors to meld together. Lasagna is celebrated for its comforting, indulgent qualities, making it a beloved staple in both traditional Italian cuisine and home-cooked meals around the world.

Lasagna is a delectable, layered Italian dish that begins with the preparation of its key components: the pasta, the sauce, and the cheese filling. To make a classic meat lasagna, start by creating a rich, flavorful meat sauce. This involves browning ground beef or sausage with onions and garlic, then simmering it with tomatoes, tomato paste, and a blend of Italian herbs such as basil, oregano, and thyme. Meanwhile, a creamy béchamel sauce is prepared by whisking milk into a roux made of butter and flour until it thickens, or alternatively, a ricotta mixture is made by blending ricotta cheese with eggs, parsley, and seasoning. The lasagna noodles, whether homemade or store-bought, are cooked until just tender.

Assembling the lasagna involves layering these components in a baking dish. Begin with a thin layer of sauce on the bottom, followed by a layer of lasagna noodles. Next, spread a portion of the meat sauce over the noodles, then add a layer of the béchamel or ricotta mixture, and sprinkle with a blend of shredded mozzarella and Parmesan cheese. Repeat these layers, typically three or four times, finishing with a final layer of sauce and a generous topping of cheese. The assembled lasagna is then baked in a preheated oven at 375°F (190°C) for about 45 minutes to an hour, or until the top is bubbly and golden brown. After baking, allow the lasagna to rest for a few minutes before serving to let the layers set, ensuring a perfect slice each time.

Ingredients

  • Meat Sauce

    • 1 pound (450 grams) ground beef or sausage
    • 1 onion, finely chopped
    • 3 cloves garlic, minced
    • 1 can (28 ounces/800 grams) crushed tomatoes
    • 1 can (6 ounces/170 grams) tomato paste
    • 1 can (15 ounces/425 grams) tomato sauce
    • 2 teaspoons dried basil
    • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
    • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
    • Salt and pepper to taste
    • 1 tablespoon olive oil

  • Béchamel Sauce (or Ricotta Mixture)

    • 4 tablespoons butter
    • 4 tablespoons all-purpose flour
    • 3 cups milk
    • Salt and pepper to taste
    • Pinch of nutmeg (optional)

      • OR

    • 2 cups ricotta cheese
    • 2 eggs
    • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley (or 1 tablespoon dried parsley)
    • Salt and pepper to taste

  • Lasagna Assembly

    • 12 lasagna noodles (cooked according to package instructions)
    • 4 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
    • 1 cup grated Parmesan cheese

  • Optional

  • Fresh basil or parsley for garnish

Let's make it now!

  • Step 1: Prepare the Meat Sauce

    1. Brown the Meat: In a large skillet, heat 1 tablespoon of olive oil over medium heat. Add 1 pound of ground beef or sausage and cook until browned, breaking it up with a spoon as it cooks.
    2. Cook the Aromatics: Add 1 finely chopped onion and cook until softened, about 5 minutes. Add 3 minced garlic cloves and cook for another 1-2 minutes.
    3. Simmer the Sauce: Stir in 1 can (28 ounces) of crushed tomatoes, 1 can (6 ounces) of tomato paste, and 1 can (15 ounces) of tomato sauce. Add 2 teaspoons of dried basil, 1 teaspoon of dried oregano, 1 teaspoon of dried thyme, and salt and pepper to taste. Simmer the sauce on low heat for about 30 minutes, stirring occasionally.

  • Step 2: Prepare the Béchamel Sauce (or Ricotta Mixture)

    1. Make the Béchamel Sauce: In a medium saucepan, melt 4 tablespoons of butter over medium heat. Add 4 tablespoons of all-purpose flour and whisk constantly for about 2 minutes. Gradually whisk in 3 cups of milk, continuing to whisk until the mixture is smooth and thickened, about 5-7 minutes. Season with salt, pepper, and a pinch of nutmeg if using.

      2. OR

    2. Make the Ricotta Mixture: In a bowl, mix 2 cups of ricotta cheese with 2 eggs, 1/4 cup of chopped fresh parsley (or 1 tablespoon dried parsley), and salt and pepper to taste.

  • Step 3: Cook the Lasagna Noodles

    1. Cook the Noodles: Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add 12 lasagna noodles and cook according to package instructions until al dente. Drain and lay them flat on a baking sheet to prevent sticking.

  • Step 4: Assemble the Lasagna

    1. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).

    2. Layer the Ingredients:In a 9x13 inch baking dish, spread a thin layer of meat sauce on the bottom.

      • Place a layer of lasagna noodles over the sauce.
      • Spread a layer of the meat sauce over the noodles.
      • Add a layer of béchamel or ricotta mixture over the meat sauce.
      • Sprinkle a portion of shredded mozzarella and grated Parmesan cheese over the cheese mixture.
    3. Repeat Layers:Repeat the layers (noodles, meat sauce, béchamel or ricotta, and cheeses) until you have 3-4 layers, ending with a final layer of sauce and a generous topping of cheese.

  • Step 5: Bake the Lasagna

    1. Cover and Bake: Cover the baking dish with aluminum foil and bake in the preheated oven for about 45 minutes.
    2. Uncover and Continue Baking: Remove the foil and bake for an additional 15 minutes, or until the top is golden and bubbly.

  • Step 6: Rest and Serve

    1. Rest: Let the lasagna rest for about 10-15 minutes before slicing. This helps the layers set and makes it easier to serve.
    2. Garnish and Enjoy: Garnish with fresh basil or parsley if desired, and enjoy your delicious homemade lasagna!
